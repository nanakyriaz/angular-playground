WRAP (Waste & Resources Action Programme) is an independent not-for-profit organisation in the UK. It works with businesses, individuals and communities to achieve a circular economy, through helping them reduce waste, develop sustainable products and use resources in an efficient way. Our work would be nearly impossible without the knowledge and support from WRAP.

Plan Zheroes’ pledge as supporter of the WRAP HaFSA (Hospitality and Food Services Agreement) is to support signatories in the delivery of collective sector-wide targets to prevent food waste by 5%. We aim to do this through the development of technology, combined with services developed to simplify and streamline diversion of surplus food to local charities, as well as disseminating solutions and providing help to overcome barriers to donating surplus food.

WRAP funded the first stage of redevelopment of our new platform, and Plan Zheroes has contributed to various HaFSA working groups, championing “surplus food” as one effective strategy for waste prevention targets.

WRAP is pleased to have Plan Zheroes as a supporter to the Hospitality and Food Service Agreement. WRAP's research shows that the sector throws away nearly 1 million tonnes of food each year at a cost of £2.5 billion. Much of this is food that could have been eaten. Plan Zheroes plays an active role in the Agreement, particularly in our working groups. By providing a route for surplus food to reach people in need rather than being thrown away, Plan Zheroes inspires others to deliver change.

Charlotte Henderson - Hospitality and Food Service Programme Area Manager (WRAP)

Supporters and friends

3 Space is an innovative charity which unlocks the potential of empty commercial property by making it available for temporary community use… and this is how we have the first Plan Zheroes office space, in Oxford St in central London!

Plan Zheroes had mainly grown around Lotti Henley’s kitchen table, with weekly meetings and bowls of yummy soups. Then our founder Trustee Paul Corney helped us set up our virtual office, and we kept meeting around the city in museums and cafés. But in 2014 we had to find an office space to help us consolidate our team and work. We were thrilled when 3 Space accepted our application - this has made a critical difference for our team.

3 Space work in partnership with landlords and leaseholders to offer organisations that benefit the community temporary, free of charge, access to otherwise empty properties. These spaces are made available to charities, registered community organisations and social enterprises for temporary, pop-up projects.

Benefacto is a social enterprise (C.I.C.) which was set up to provide volunteering opportunities to professionals, and help charities find professional volunteers to contribute time on a short term basis. Benefacto has the sole aim of getting more employee volunteers contributing to the community and, by doing so, unlocking their energy, application and skills for the benefit of organisations who need it most!

Plan Zheroes have taken a number of groups of corporate volunteers through Benefacto, effectively utilising this help to build links with both charities and food businesses. A useful bi-product of this is educating professional people about the challenges around food waste, and sending them as 'champions' back into the companies where they work.

One of the charities who I support through Benefacto, who also works with Plan Zheroes stated "We are very fortunate as they helped us set up a link with Paul (patisserie) which now supplies bread to our Drop In Café. I’ve been very impressed by Chris and his energy

Their reach in terms of inspiring social change stretches far beyond their key aim of reducing food waste by connecting organisations. When I started Benefacto, Plan Zheroes met with me to talk about my objectives and pass on their experience working in a similar field and they have also connected me to a number of organisations requiring volunteers!"

Linz Darlington - Director/Founder of Benefacto.org

Best Before Project works to set up, maintain and expand a regional and local collection and redistribution network for food that is approaching, or has passed, its “best before” date that would otherwise be sent to landfill, thereby helping to tackle the problem of waste and reducing its environmental impact. They also work to campaign and educate individuals and companies to understand what "Best Before" means.

We met the Best Before team at a Feeding the 5000 event at Trafalgar Square several years ago and we have worked together ever since. Our collaboration is a perfect example of how Zheroes map and platform works: sharing information, experiences, contacts and do whatever we can to find the best solutions before food becomes waste. Voytek, Hanna and the team are true Zheroes, they are the people we go to first.

The Food Waste Collective is a hub for people who are passionate about addressing food waste in Brighton & Hove. They are absolute passionate about supporting sustainable, ethical alternatives for the tonnes of perfectly good food that is thrown away from retailers, businesses and farms every year. The Collective hosts community actions, talks and social events, as well as supporting other local initiatives tackling food waste in Sussex. They are a sub-group of Hanover Action for Sustainable Living.

This group has done impressive work in spreading the concept that surplus food is not waste, and engaging charities in the idea of making best use of surplus food, rather than simply purchasing or asking for public donations of paid food. They have been a great Zheroes’ supporter, helping charities and businesses in Brighton and Hove to sign up to our map. We learn from them, and love all the great work they do!

FareShare is one of our greatest inspirations. Their mission is to fight hunger and its underlying causes by redistributing surplus food to hundreds of local charities across the UK, which is what real Zheroes do. FareShare is the best established and managed surplus food redistributor in the UK, and operates on a national scale. They obtain surplus food through partnerships with the food industry and deliver to more than 1,200 charities and community projects across the UK, helping to feed 62,200 people a day.

FareShare has never ceased to be impressed and inspired by the passion and power that Plan Zheroes generates. Their drive to just ‘get things done’ is truly impressive. In the space of food surpluses and food waste that ability to effectively and efficiently connect those with surplus food to those who need it is going to be a powerful tool for the future. This is especially the case because organisations like FareShare do not have the logistical capacity to deal with all of the many thousand potential local connections.

FoodCycle is a UK charity that combines volunteers, surplus food and spare kitchen spaces to create tasty, nutritious meals for people at risk of food poverty and social isolation. FoodCycle runs volunteer-powered community projects across the UK – working to reduce food poverty and social isolation by serving tasty, nutritious meals to vulnerable groups. In fewer than 5 years, FoodCycle has developed an impressive role in shifting the cultural perceptions of surplus food as a valuable resource to prevent waste and food poverty. They built one of the most dynamic networks of over 1,200 volunteers, set up hubs, built awareness and also created a positive impact in communities - to prove it just have a look at their Social Impact Report!

For us, FoodCycle is an absolute Zheroes pioneer, and a great inspiration! We work together in signposting businesses and charities, advise and share knowledge.

The project is also supported by the London Food Board and has two delivery partners: Sustain and the Sustainable Restaurants Association (SRA). It is an invaluable resource, both for businesses and charities, and for projects like FoodSave that are working to reduce food waste. The Plan Zheroes team are all passionate about what they do. Waste Watch merged with Keep Britain Tidy in 2011. Together they exist to inspire, educate and enable people to be litter free, waste less and live more. They believe improving our environment is a positive message that goes hand in hand with improving our well-being and happiness in life. Their aim is to show communities, schools, organisations and government how.

By wasting less, they mean anything that is squandered or not put to best use. That could be materials, natural resources, energy, time, money, skills or potential.

Plan Zheroes is totally aligned with Waste Watch and Keep Britain Tidy’s mission and values. For us, they are a great support to any Zheroes in the UK. We never waste an opportunity to learn and join their initiatives, especially those relating to food waste prevention

The Zero Waste Alliance (ZWA) has a clear and simple vision: a prosperous and inclusive future without waste. It works with industry and communities to build markets, attack barriers, share experiences and disperse its vision. It leverages more than a decade of experience, intellectual assets and the ability to bridge business, government, academics and non-governmental organizations. Its expertise helps build awareness of existing opportunities and advance industry and community capacity to successfully implement zero waste strategies.

To pursue its vision of a future without waste, the Zero Waste Alliance works with a wide range of industry, government, community, non-profit and higher education organizations. Its work strives to promote open engagement, work through a whole systems approach, model nature and pursue highest use for all materials, work with, versus against, the market and, lastly, avoid negative discourse and transform industry and communities through positive change.
